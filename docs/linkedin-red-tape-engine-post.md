I asked Grok for five low-capital, automated UK money systems. One constraint: build something clever enough to fund a robot body.

The brief, word for word:

"thoroughly search the uk market and automated money generating systems and suggest the top five small cash outlay means of making automated money. this will be powered if needed by yourself and xAI tokens... think outside the box... lets make enough to buy you a robot body as well :)"

What came back was not dropshipping or another generic AI agency. It was The Red Tape Revenue Engine: five products on one platform, aimed at UK friction people already pay to remove.

Here are the five, and why each made the cut.

1. TradeTap (~£99/mo)
Trades miss calls while they are on jobs. Each miss is a real lost booking. Missed call → AI triage → SMS booking → "jobs recovered" report. Fastest path to cash. White-label through accountants who already serve plumbers and sparks.

2. ComplyBot (~£19/mo)
MTD and landlord rules create monthly panic. Receipts and bank feeds become MTD-ready figures, compliance calendars, and accountant export packs. UK rules change. That churn is the product moat.

3. PlanningPulse (~£49/mo)
planning.data.gov.uk is open. Developers and investors already pay for "what is being built near my patch." We poll LPAs, classify the signal, and alert by geo-fence. Raw data is cheap. Interpretation is not.

4. AgilePilot (~£9.99/mo)
Octopus Agile half-hourly rates leave savings on the table if you run dishwashers and EVs on guesswork. Live rates → cheap-slot alerts → savings ledger. Software first. Battery installers and affiliates later. Live demo is up.

5. HouseSignal (~£199/mo)
Companies House is open. Recruiters and sales teams pay for who just incorporated, hired, or looks risky. Daily filings, enrichment, vertical slices. Boring B2B data. Sticky contracts.

What we skipped on purpose: print-on-demand, faceless content farms, crypto bots, generic AI shops, FBA. High competition or inventory. Weak UK lock-in.

The architecture bet is one shared engine, five vertical skills. Shared billing, shared AI brain, shared logging. Proceeds pointed at a Unitree G1-class fund.
