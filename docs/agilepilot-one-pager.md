## The opportunity

Retailers already own tariffs, meters, and billing. What they still struggle to scale is **device-level load-shifting** across EV chargers, batteries, heat pumps, and home appliances — with **auditable savings** customers trust.

Building every OEM integration and every multi-tariff policy engine inside core billing platforms is slow and risky. Partners who specialise in **control-plane software** ship faster without touching meter-to-cash.

---

## What Capstone offers

**A multi-tariff load-shifting control plane** that energy brands can white-label:

| Capability | What it does |
|------------|----------------|
| **Tariff adapters** | Normalised half-hourly rates across suppliers (Octopus Agile-class, EDF open tariffs, more) |
| **Shift policies** | “Charge EV by 07:00”, comfort constraints, peak avoid, green-prefer |
| **Savings ledger** | Baseline vs shifted kWh with clear attribution for customers and regulators |
| **Multi-tenant SaaS** | White-label per brand / Kraken licensee; isolated tenants |
| **OEM-ready packaging** | NDA, DPA path, security documentation, EU hosting |

**We do not replace billing platforms.** We sit beside them.

---

## Why partner (not build in-house)

1. **Not core to meter-to-cash** — device ecosystems change monthly; billing must not.  
2. **Multi-supplier by design** — same control plane across Kraken-family open APIs and beyond.  
3. **Faster time-to-feature** for licensees who need differentiated smart energy UX.  
4. **Risk isolation** — partner product can iterate without endangering billing SLAs.  
5. **Procurement-ready** — security baseline, data minimisation, clear IP licensing.

## Architecture (high level)

```
Tariff providers (Octopus, EDF, …)  →  Capstone control plane  →  White-label API / app
Device hooks (future)               →  policy + savings ledger →  Customer outcomes
```

Public rate APIs are inputs — **not** the product. The product is orchestration, attribution, and multi-tenant delivery.
